# Traversal Algorithms

> Systematically visit every node, cell, or element of a structure, choosing a strategy (DFS, BFS, or order-specific) that fits the shape and the question.

> **Use when**
> - You must reach every node of a tree/graph or every cell of a grid, and want each visited once.
> - You need shortest path or minimum steps in an unweighted graph/grid (BFS expands by distance).
> - You explore deep paths, count connected components, or detect cycles (DFS recursion or an explicit stack).
> - You must order tasks under prerequisite constraints (topological sort).
> - The traversal order itself matters: inorder of a BST, level order, spiral, diagonal, or boundary.

> **Complexity**
> - Tree/graph DFS or BFS: **O(V + E)** time; grid variants are **O(rows · cols)**.
> - Recursion/queue stack: **O(V)** space (worst-case **O(H)** for balanced-tree DFS); Morris traversal is **O(1)** space.

## Related patterns

- **Binary Search** — a specific form of array traversal that halves the search space.
- **Two Pointers** — often used in conjunction with linear traversal.
- **Dynamic Programming** — many DP problems rely on a specific traversal order.
- **Graph Algorithms** — most build on DFS/BFS traversals as their foundation.
