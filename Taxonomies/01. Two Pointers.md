# Two Pointers

> Two indices move over a sequence to replace nested loops, cutting many O(n²) scans down to O(n).

> **Use when**
> - The data is sorted (or you can sort it) and you need a pair/triplet meeting a sum or closeness target.
> - You converge from both ends: palindromes, container/area, reversing in place.
> - You rewrite an array in place: dedup, partition, move/overwrite (a read pointer + a write pointer).
> - You need a linked-list cycle, middle, or k-th-from-end (a fast + a slow pointer).
> - You scan a contiguous window over an array/string (a sliding window is same-direction two pointers).

> **Complexity**
> - One pass, opposite or same direction: **O(n)** time, **O(1)** extra space.
> - k-Sum that sorts first (3Sum/4Sum): **O(n log n)** to sort, then **O(n^(k-1))** for the nested sweeps.

## Related patterns

- **Binary Search** — controls pointer movement via midpoint checks.
- **Sliding Window** — a same-direction specialization.
- **Sorting** — the merge step is a two-pointer sweep; k-Sum sorts first.
- **Linked List** — cycle detection, middle finding, k-th-from-end.
