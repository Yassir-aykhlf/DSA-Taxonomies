# Bit Manipulation

> Operate directly on the binary representation of integers with bitwise operators to count, toggle, mask, and combine bits in constant time per word.

> **Use when**
> - You set, clear, toggle, or test an individual bit, or count how many bits are set.
> - You XOR a stream to cancel duplicates and isolate a unique or missing number.
> - You represent a small set of items as a bitmask and iterate subsets or track visited states (bitmask DP).
> - You replace arithmetic with shifts and adds: multiply/divide by powers of two, add without `+`.
> - You exploit identities like `n & (n-1)` (clear lowest set bit) or `n & -n` (isolate lowest set bit).

> **Complexity**
> - Per-bit operations on a fixed-width integer: **O(1)**; scanning all bits of a w-bit word: **O(w)** (≈32).
> - Bitmask DP over n items: **O(2^n · n)** time, **O(2^n)** space.

## Related patterns

- **Dynamic Programming** — bitmask DP encodes subsets/visited states as integers.
- **Math** — many arithmetic operations have faster bitwise formulations.
- **Hash Table** — bitmasks act as compact, hashable set keys (DNA sequences, puzzles).
- **Trie** — binary tries solve maximum-XOR problems bit by bit.
- **Backtracking** — bit flags track chosen elements while enumerating subsets.
