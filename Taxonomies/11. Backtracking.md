# Backtracking

> Incrementally build a solution by choosing an option, exploring deeper, and undoing the choice when a path fails — a guided DFS over the space of partial solutions.

> **Use when**
> - You must enumerate all subsets, combinations, permutations, or partitions of a set.
> - You generate every valid string/arrangement under rules (parentheses, IP addresses, phone letters).
> - A constraint must hold at each step and invalid branches can be pruned early (N-Queens, Sudoku, word search on a grid).
> - You search a graph or matrix for all paths/colorings, marking and unmarking cells as you go.
> - The full search space is exponential but pruning (and sometimes memoization) makes it tractable.

> **Complexity**
> - Subsets: **O(n · 2^n)**; permutations: **O(n · n!)** — output-bound, one factor for copying each result.
> - Constraint search (N-Queens, Sudoku): exponential worst case, drastically cut by pruning.
> - Space: **O(n)** recursion depth beyond the output (plus the board/visited state).

## Related patterns

- **Depth-First Search** — backtracking is a DFS that undoes choices on the way back up.
- **Dynamic Programming** — caching overlapping subproblems turns backtracking into memoized DP.
- **Recursion** — the structural foundation every backtracking routine builds on.
- **Bit Manipulation** — bitmasks compactly track used elements or board states during the search.
