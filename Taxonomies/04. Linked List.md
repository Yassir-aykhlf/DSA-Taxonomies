# Linked List

> Nodes chained by pointers: rewiring links gives O(1) insertion and deletion, at the cost of no random access.

> **Use when**
> - You insert or delete in the middle without shifting elements, so a dummy head simplifies edge cases.
> - You reverse a list, in whole or in a sublist / k-group, by rewiring `next` pointers.
> - You need a cycle, the middle, or the k-th-from-end node (fast & slow pointers).
> - You merge, partition, or split lists by splicing nodes rather than copying.
> - You build a structure on top of nodes: stack/queue, LRU/LFU cache, browser history.

> **Complexity**
> - Traverse / search / length: **O(n)** time, **O(1)** space.
> - Insert or delete at a known node: **O(1)**; reverse whole or sublist: **O(n)** time, **O(1)** space.
> - Merge sort on a list: **O(n log n)** time, **O(log n)** stack space.

## Related patterns

- **Two Pointers** — fast/slow and runner alignment over nodes.
- **Recursion** — mirrors pointer unwinding for list operations.
- **Merge Sort** — stable ordering tailored to node merges.
- **Stack & Queue** — iterative substitutes for recursive traversals.
