# Combinatorics & Number Theory

> Mathematical tools for counting arrangements and reasoning about integer properties — permutations, combinations, primes, and modular arithmetic.

> **Use when**
> - You must count or enumerate permutations, combinations, or arrangements (with or without duplicates).
> - The answer is a huge count that must be returned modulo 1e9+7, needing modular arithmetic and inverses.
> - You work with primes, factorization, or GCD/LCM properties of numbers.
> - You compute large powers or factorials efficiently (fast exponentiation, factorial-zero counting).
> - You analyze a turn-based game for a winning strategy (Nim, minimax, Grundy numbers).

> **Complexity**
> - Sieve of Eratosthenes: **O(n log log n)**; Euclidean GCD: **O(log min(a, b))**.
> - Fast modular exponentiation: **O(log n)**; nCr with precomputed factorials: **O(1)** after **O(n)** prep.
